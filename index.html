Apr 28 · LLMP useful — grab Peter Pan and Jungle Cruise return times first

Fantasyland
Start here — best land for ages 3–5
Peter Pan's Flight
get LL

Top LL priority. Line hits 60–90 min and barely moves. Slightly dark but not scary — one of the most magical rides at WDW for this age.

Enchanted Tales with Belle
don't skip

Interactive show — one kid gets chosen to play a character alongside Belle. Best experience in any park for ages 3–5. Adults enjoy this one just as much.

Winnie the Pooh

Gentle and charming. The 3yo will love it. 20–30 min wait manageable without LL.

Dumbo the Flying Elephant

Two loading areas keep wait under 20 min. Kids control up/down themselves. No LL needed.

it's a small world

Slow, calm, colorful. Worth doing once. Walk-on in the evening.

Little Mermaid ride

Often near walk-on. Good if you're already passing through.

Princess Fairytale Hall

Meet Cinderella and Rapunzel. Budget 30–45 min. Prioritize if your kids are into princesses.

Mad Tea Party

Spinning teacups. Gauge their tolerance first — some kids love it, some regret it immediately.

Pinocchio Village Haus
lunch

Kids → pasta and pizza. Adults → the flatbreads are the best option here. Not a destination meal but the window overlooks it's a small world boats floating past — unexpectedly fun while you eat.

Gaston's Tavern
snack

Kids → LeFou's Brew (frozen apple juice slush) and the enormous cinnamon roll to share. Adults → the slow-roasted pork shank is a surprisingly solid snack — far more interesting than the cinnamon roll and worth trying.

Adventureland
Jungle Cruise
get LL

Second LL priority. The skipper's terrible jokes land perfectly with kids — adults groan along in the best way. 25–40 min without LL.

Magic Carpets of Aladdin

Like Dumbo. Short wait. Good filler while in Adventureland.

Pirates of the Caribbean

Dark — the 3yo may be nervous at first but usually adjusts. Twins will love it. Line moves fast, no LL needed.

Dole Whip
snack

Aloha Isle. Kids → classic pineapple soft serve. Adults → get the Dole Whip float — pineapple juice with soft serve on top. One of the best things in the park for everyone, full stop.

Tomorrowland
Buzz Lightyear Space Ranger Spin

Shooter game with individual blasters. Kids take the competition very seriously. Wait rarely over 25 min.

Tomorrowland Speedway

Kids feel like they're driving. Skip if wait is over 20 min.

Monsters Inc. Laugh Floor

Live interactive comedy show. Great A/C break, walk in anytime. Funnier for adults than expected.

Cosmic Ray's Starlight Cafe
lunch

Kids → chicken strips, mac & cheese, kids' burgers. Adults → skip the burgers — get the rotisserie chicken. Fastest option if you're on the Tomorrowland side of the park.

Liberty Square
Haunted Mansion

Spooky but family-friendly. Gauge your 3yo first. Twins will love it. Adults consistently rank this a favorite. LL only if wait tops 40 min.

Columbia Harbour House
lunch

Best quick service at MK. Kids → chicken strips, mac & cheese, grilled cheese. Adults → the New England clam chowder and lobster roll are legitimately excellent. Go upstairs — quieter seating, easier with little kids.

Main Street, U.S.A.
Mickey & Minnie meet & greet

Town Square Theater. Go at rope drop before waits build. Probably the photo you frame.

Festival of Fantasy Parade
don't miss

Runs once daily ~3pm. Grab a Main Street spot 30 min early. Kids this age go completely wild for it.

Main Street Bakery
snack

It's a Starbucks. Kids → cake pops, hot chocolate. Adults → the only real coffee in Magic Kingdom. Get here right at park open before the line builds. Non-negotiable with three kids under 6.

Mickey Premium Ice Cream Bar
snack

Any cart. Works for everyone. Budget one per kid and don't pretend you won't get one yourself.

Popcorn with Mickey bucket
snack

Main Street cart. Get the refillable bucket — free refills at every popcorn cart across all three parks all trip. Pays for itself fast with three kids.

Apr 29 · Fewest kid rides of the three parks — lean into food and World Showcase. Adults can carry drinks freely anywhere around the showcase.

World Nature
Living with the Land

Slow boat through real working greenhouses. Calm and low wait. Adults find this more interesting than expected.

Turtle Talk with Crush

Live interactive show — Crush takes audience questions in real time. Kids are mesmerized. Adults laugh just as hard. Walk-in anytime.

Sunshine Seasons
lunch

Most varied quick service at EPCOT. Kids → chicken strips, mac & cheese, noodle bowls. Adults → the oak-grilled fish and roasted chicken are excellent — one of the better QS meals at WDW overall.

World Showcase · Norway
Rope drop Frozen Ever After before LLs sell out
Frozen Ever After
get LL

Most in-demand at EPCOT — LLs sell out by 9:30am. If no LLMP, this is your rope drop target.

Anna & Elsa meet & greet

Royal Sommerhus. 30–45 min wait. Worth it for Frozen fans, skip otherwise.

Kringla Bakeri og Kafe
snack

Kids → school bread (cardamom custard roll with coconut) — the iconic EPCOT snack; get one per person. Adults → the open-faced gravlax sandwich and rice cream are worth trying. Norway also has aquavit shots for adults who want a drink while the kids eat.

World Showcase · France
Remy's Ratatouille Adventure
get LL

Best ride at EPCOT for this age group — shrink-down kitchen adventure. 45–60 min without LL. Essential.

Les Halles Boulangerie-Patisserie
lunch

Best lunch at EPCOT. Kids → ham and cheese crepes, kids' sandwiches, pastries. Adults → the French onion soup, croque monsieur, and a glass of wine — the most legitimately adult lunch in any Disney park. Go early; pastries sell out.

Les Halles pastries
snack

Kids → croissant, pain au chocolat. Adults → the croissant donut and eclairs are excellent. Grab snacks here at the same time as lunch — one trip through the line.

World Showcase · Mexico
Gran Fiesta Tour

Slow boat through the Mexico pavilion pyramid. Nearly always walk-on. Good A/C rest break.

La Cantina de San Angel
lunch

Outdoor seating on the World Showcase lagoon. Kids → tacos, quesadillas, empanadas. Adults → margaritas and tacos with a lagoon view. This is EPCOT — adults can carry drinks anywhere in the showcase.

World Showcase · Morocco
No rides — food stop only
Tangierine Cafe
lunch

Consistently underrated with almost always short lines. Kids → chicken tenders, hummus and pita. Adults → the chicken and lamb shawarma platter with hummus and tabouleh is one of the best value meals in all of World Showcase.

World Celebration
Journey Into Imagination with Figment

Kids enjoy Figment. Good A/C filler, low wait.

Disney & Pixar Short Film Festival

4D film show. Walk-in anytime. Best used when someone needs to sit and cool off.

Frozen lemonade or slush
snack

Various kiosks around World Showcase. Kids → the lemon slush is a reliable hit. Adults → most kiosks offer spiked versions. Essential for managing late April heat for everyone.

Go to Kilimanjaro Safaris the moment the park opens. Animals most active in the morning.

Africa
Start here at rope drop
Kilimanjaro Safaris
rope drop first

Real animals, open vehicles — lions, elephants, giraffes, hippos. No LL needed at park open. Best single thing in AK for kids and adults equally.

Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail

Self-guided trail — gorillas, hippos, exotic birds. Low-energy and good right after Safaris while everyone is still in animal mode.

Harambe Market
lunch

Convenient right off Safaris. Kids → chicken skewers, corn, kids' options. Adults → the ribs and slow-roasted chicken are solid. Dawa Bar is steps away — adults can grab a Tusker Lager or African-spiced cocktail while the kids eat.

Pandora — World of Avatar
Na'vi River Journey
get LL

Beautiful, calming boat through bioluminescent forest. LL fills fast — grab it early. Genuinely stunning for adults and kids alike.

Satu'li Canteen
lunch

Best food in the park. Kids → customizable bowls; mac & cheese base is a reliable win for picky eaters. Adults → the grain bowls with roasted meat are excellent and genuinely filling — healthiest QS at any Disney park.

Pongu Pongu
snack

Kids → Night Blossom — gorgeous layered blue-green drink; kids love the look as much as the taste. Adults → the alcoholic version is available and equally worth getting. The pineapple cream cheese lumpia is also an underrated adult snack.

Shows
Don't skip either of these
Festival of the Lion King
must see

Spectacular live show — acrobatics, singing, fire. All three kids will be transfixed. Adults will be genuinely moved. Check showtimes in the app; arrive 15 min early. Do not leave AK without seeing this.

Finding Nemo: The Big Blue...and Beyond!

Musical show with characters. Good for kids. Nice break during the hottest part of the day.

DinoLand U.S.A.
TriceraTop Spin

Like Dumbo but dinosaur-themed. Reliable short wait. Good low-key ride when kids need to reset.

Discovery Island
It's Tough to be a Bug
avoid

No height requirement but terrifying for young children — dark, very loud, bugs spray and sting the audience. Skip entirely with a 3yo regardless of what the sign says.

Tree of Life gardens

Walk around and spot all the animals carved into the tree. No wait. Good transition between areas.

Flame Tree BBQ
lunch

Best full meal in any Disney park. Kids → chicken and mac & cheese kids' plates. Adults → the pulled pork sandwich, St. Louis ribs, and smoked chicken are all genuinely excellent. Outdoor waterfront seating. If you pick one sit-down lunch all trip, make it this one.

Creature Comforts
snack

It's a Starbucks. Kids → cake pops, hot chocolate. Adults → the only proper coffee at Animal Kingdom. Central location means you'll pass it multiple times — use it.

Carrot cake cookie
snack

Creature Comforts. Cream cheese frosting shaped like a carrot. Kids → will fight over it. Adults → get two. Soft, rich, one of the best snacks in any of the three parks.

Mickey pretzel
snack

Various carts near Discovery Island. Good for everyone — holds kids over between meals without a sugar crash.
